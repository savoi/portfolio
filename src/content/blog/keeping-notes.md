Publishing in-progress thinking has been one of the most useful ways to make learning stick. Instead of hoarding notes in private notebooks, I've started to share artefacts as they happen: audio snippets, moodboards, prompts, even half-finished prototypes.

## Lightweight workflows

1. **Capture ruthlessly.** Voice memos, quick sketches, terminal recordingsâ€”anything ephemeral gets saved to a "scratch" folder.
2. **Triage weekly.** I set aside time to re-read and tag what still feels interesting.
3. **Publish tiny.** Not everything needs a full essay. A paragraph and a couple of links often does the job.

## Share the process

Artefacts aren't just outputs; they document the journey. By making them public, collaborators can peek into evolving ideas and offer feedback before things calcify.
